Ethical Issues Facing Engineers and their Profession

In the article Ethical Issues Facing Engineers and their Profession, the authors discuss the ethical considerations in engineering. They explore the following topics:

  • Current perceptions of ethics among engineers.
  • Obstacles faced in promoting ethical conduct.
  • The role of professional societies, like IEEE and ABET, in establishing and updating ethical standards.
"The article emphasizes the importance of integrating ethics education into engineering curricula to better prepare young engineers for the ethical difficulties they may encounter in their careers."

This is a great article where we can learn about the current environment of ethics in engineering. It provides many examples for a better understanding, and while reflecting on the mistakes that caused large problems in the past, it offers valuable lessons.

Key Points from the Article:

  • Case studies: Engineering schools can analyze real-world problems through case studies, allowing students to engage with real data.
  • Dynamic ethics: Engineering ethics must evolve with technological advancements. It's crucial to update the curriculum to reflect this.

This is especially true for older engineering schools like Purdue. I recall our dean mentioning that many institutions founded centuries ago face challenges in adapting to modern trends.

"We have AI now, and I believe our school is doing great in leading this new trend with updated policies and integrating AI into the learning experience."

Overall, the article makes a strong case for continuous updates in ethics education, ensuring that young engineers are equipped to handle new challenges.
